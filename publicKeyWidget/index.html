It's close. In the real thing (RSA encryption) the private and public keys are numbers, but they are exponents used on numbers rather than just plain multiplication. The modulo part is still the same. The computationally hard problem you have to solve to crack the key is also a little different: rather than simply finding the multiplicative inverse, you have to find the prime factors of a very large number which is actually even more computationally unreasonable.
